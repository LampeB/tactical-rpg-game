RPG2 / Design System

Color & Surface

Warm paper · ink · brass
Tokens prefixed --
OKLCH-defined · sRGB-fallback

Surfaces — Paper

Warm off-white tones for backgrounds, panels, and inset surfaces. The deeper paper variants build hierarchy without resorting to gray. Use paper for the canvas, paper-2 for raised panels, paper-3 for inset surfaces (grid wells, empty slots).

Type — Ink

Four tiers of warm near-black for text and strokes. Each step is a measurable ~12–14% lightness apart, so the eye reads them as discrete weights, not a gradient. Reserve ink-4 for tertiary affordances; never use it for body copy.

Accents

Five hues at controlled lightness/chroma so they pair without clashing. Brass is the system's principal accent (selection, primary CTAs, headings). Ember is for live/heat states — the forge arrow, danger destroy buttons, "ready" feedback. Moss for craftable / success / health. Indigo for MP and quiet secondary affordances.

Rarity — Item Tiers

Six tiers at matched lightness (~0.55–0.62) so no rarity reads as more "important" than another by brightness alone — the eye distinguishes them by hue. Used on item silhouettes, the rarity chip (top-right of slots), and the rarity ladder (bottom of slots).

Status — Feedback Colors

Reserved colors for system-level state, separate from the accent palette. Success is the moss-green of "this is ready / this worked" — used by the forge output card, the "ready to forge" indicator, and craftable-recipe pips. Success-bright is its higher-chroma pair, used only for live attention (pulsing borders, glows).

Stage — Page Backdrop

The dark frame around the 1920×1080 canvas. These never touch UI content — they exist to letterbox the design when the viewport doesn't match aspect ratio. Two-stop radial gradient from stage-warm at center to stage-deep at edges.

Special Purposes

Single-use tokens reserved for specific contexts. Glyph-ink is the dark ink color used for item glyphs (✦ ◈ ⌇ …) painted on top of saturated rarity silhouettes — pure --ink reads too dark on bright fills. Modal-veil is the dimming layer behind dialogs.

Gems — Modifier Hues

Five elemental hues for placed gems on the Lattice. The colors are deliberately a hair brighter than the rarity palette (slightly higher chroma) so a gem reads as "energy" against an item's "matter." Use the gem hue for reach-cell overlays, modified-item halos, and gem-inspector accents.

Usage rules

  1. Lightness controls hierarchy, hue carries meaning. All rarity tiers share lightness ≈ 0.55–0.62 so the eye reads tier by hue, not brightness. Never lighten or darken a rarity hue to "show importance."
  2. Brass = selection, ember = action. A selected item gets a brass ring; a button you actually press gets ember. Don't conflate them — your CTA shouldn't quietly steal the selection language.
  3. Moss is reserved for craftable / success. Don't use moss for HP bars decoratively; it carries semantic weight.
  4. Gem hues sit on top of items, never under them. If a gem hue appears as a background, demote it heavily (color-mix at ~6–12%) so item rarity always wins visually.
  5. Paper-3 is the deepest paper allowed in body content. Anything darker reads as ink and breaks the warmth.
  6. Hairlines for division, ink for decision. Use hairline between metadata rows; reserve full 1.5–2px ink borders for things the player can touch or commit on.
